Startups often require wearing many hats. Can you describe a project where you had to lead an initiative outside of your core job description? How did you manage the lack of established guidelines?

In my startup, I was the sole engineer but stepped up to lead sales outreach when we needed to close our first paying customers. With no established sales process, I created a simple framework: researched target companies, crafted personalized cold emails based on their pain points, tracked responses in a shared Google Sheet, and iterated weekly based on what converted.

I managed the ambiguity by setting small milestones (10 emails/day, 20% response goal), learning from rejections, and borrowing playbooks from successful SaaS founders I'd studied. We landed 3 customers in 6 weeks, which funded our next dev hires. This taught me to break big unknowns into testable steps and stay customer-focused when guidelines don't exist.
